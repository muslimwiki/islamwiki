# Islam

**Islam**<ref>/ˈɪzlɑːm, ˈɪzlæm/ *IZ-la(h)m*; Arabic: ٱلْإِسْلَام‎, <small>romanized:</small> *al-Islām*, IPA: [alʔɪsˈlaːm], <abbr>lit.</abbr> 'submission [to the will of God]'</ref> is the final and complete way of life revealed by [[Allah]] (ﷻ) for all of humanity, and it is followed by over 1.9 billion people across the world who are known as **[[Muslims]]**.<ref>"[https://www.pewresearch.org/topic/religion/religious-demographics/pew-templeton-global-religious-futures-project/ Pew-Templeton Global Religious Futures Project - Research and data from Pew Research Center]". 21 December 2022.</ref>

{{Cquote|Indeed, the religion in the sight of Allah is Islam.|{{qref|3|19|b=yl|c=y|y=si}}}}

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ did not bring a new religion, but came to confirm and complete the message of all previous prophets, including **[[Noah]] (Nūḥ), [[Abraham]] (Ibrāhīm), [[Moses]] (Mūsā), and [[Jesus]] (ʿĪsā)**—peace be upon them all.

{{Cquote|Say, 'We believe in Allah and what has been revealed to us and what was revealed to Abraham, Ishmael, Isaac, Jacob, and the Descendants, and what was given to Moses and Jesus and what was given to the prophets from their Lord. We make no distinction between any of them, and to Him we submit'|{{qref|2|136|b=yl|c=y|y=si}}}}

## Etymology
{{Further information|[[Muslims#Etymology]], [[S-L-M]]}}

The word **Islam** (Arabic: الإسلام) comes from the [[triliteral]] root **س-ل-م** (sīn-lām-mīm), which carries the meanings of submission, surrender, peace, and wholeness.

## Acts of Worship (The Five Pillars)
{{Main|Five Pillars of Islam}}
{{Further information|[[Ibadah]]}}

Islam is based on five key practices, known as the **[[Five Pillars of Islam]]**, which guide a Muslim's daily life and spiritual growth.

### Declaration of Faith (Shahada)
{{Main|Shahada}}

The *[[shahadah]]* ([[Arabic]]: الشهادة)<ref>[[Seyed Hossein Nasr|Nasr, Seyed Hossein]] (2003). pp. 3, 39, 85, 270–272. The Heart of Islam: Enduring Values for Humanity.</ref> is the fundamental declaration of faith in Islam.

{{Cquote|*ashhadu an lā ilāha illā Allāh wa ashhadu anna Muhammad rasūl Allāh* <br/>(أشهد أن لا إله إلا الله وأشهد أن محمداً رسول الله)|"I bear witness that there is no god but God, and I bear witness that Muhammad is the messenger of God."}}

### Prayer (Salah)
{{Main|Salah}}
{{Further information|[[Masjid]], [[Jumu'ah]], [[Qibla]]}}

[[File:Mohammad_Al-Amin_Mosque_during_2019_Lebanese_revolution.jpg|alt=Interior of a large mosque with ornate chandeliers and carpets.|thumb|The mosque during the 2019 Lebanese revolution]]

In Islam, prayer — known as *ṣalāh* or *ṣalāt* ([[Arabic]]: الصلاة) — is a central act of worship that fosters a direct connection between the believer and God.

### Charity (Zakat)
{{Main|Zakat}}
{{Further information|[[Sadaqah]]}}

Zakat ([[Arabic language|Arabic]]: زكاة) is one of the fundamental pillars of Islam, a sacred duty placed upon those whom Allah has blessed with wealth.

### Fasting (Sawm)
{{Main|Sawm}}
{{See also|Fasting during Ramadan}}

In Islam, fasting (*ṣawm*, [[Arabic]]: صوم) is a deeply spiritual act of worship, in which we abstain from food, drink, [[smoking]], and other physical needs from dawn until sunset.

### Pilgrimage (Hajj)
{{Main|Hajj|Umrah}}
{{See also|Holiest sites in Islam}}

The Hajj ([[Arabic]]: حج) is the sacred pilgrimage to the holy city of [[Mecca]], and it is an obligation upon every Muslim who has the physical and financial ability to perform it at least once in their lifetime.

## Articles of Faith (The Six Pillars)
{{Main|'Aqīdah|Īmān}}

The foundational beliefs of Islam, often referred to as the **Six Articles of Faith,** include:

### Oneness of God (Allah)
{{Main|Allah}}

The core belief in Islam is *[[tawḥīd]]* (Arabic: توحيد), the absolute oneness and unity of God.

### Angels
{{Main|Angels in Islam}}

In Islam, angels (*malāʾika*, Arabic: ملك) are spiritual beings mentioned in both the Qur'an and hadith.

### Prophets and Messengers
{{Main|Prophets and Messengers in Islam}}

In Islam, prophets (*[[anbiyā']]*, [[Arabic]]: أنبياء) are human beings chosen by God to convey His guidance to humanity.

### Revealed Scriptures
{{Main|Revelation in Islam|Qur'an}}
{{See also|History of the Qur'an}}

The central sacred text of Islam is the [[Qur'an]], which Muslims believe was revealed to the Prophet Muhammad by God through the archangel [[Jibrīl]] (Gabriel).

### Day of Judgment
{{Main|Yawm al-Qiyāmah|Islamic eschatology}}

Belief in the *Day of Resurrection* (*Yawm al-Qiyāmah*, [[Arabic]]: يوم القيامة) is a central tenet of Islam.

### Divine Decree (Predestination)
{{Main|Qadr}}

In Islam, the concept of divine predestination (*al-qadāʾ wa'l-qadar*, [[Arabic]]: القضاء والقدر) refers to the belief that all events occur according to God's will and decree.

## Demographics
{{See also|Islam by country}}

With an estimated population of almost 1.9 billion followers as of 2020, Muslims comprise around 25% of the world's total population.

## Misconceptions and Clarifications

Islam, like many other global religions, has been subject to widespread misconceptions. As Muslims, we are obligated to clarify these misunderstandings with truth and wisdom, as Allah ﷻ commands in the Qur'an:

> "Invite to the way of your Lord with wisdom and good instruction, and argue with them in a way that is best." (Surah An-Nahl 16:125)

### Islam and Peace

One of the most common misconceptions is that Islam promotes violence or intolerance. This is a gross distortion of the faith. The very word "Islam" comes from the root word **س ل م** (S-L-M), which encompasses meanings of peace, submission, and safety.

> "O you who have believed, enter into peace completely [and perfectly] and do not follow the footsteps of Satan. Indeed, he is to you a clear enemy." (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:208)

### The Misunderstanding of Jihad

The term **jihad** is another commonly misunderstood concept. In Islamic understanding, **jihad** literally means "struggle" or "striving." The **greater jihad (al-jihad al-akbar)** refers to the internal struggle against one's own desires, sins, and laziness in obeying Allah ﷻ.

### Women in Islam

Another frequent misconception is that Islam oppresses women. On the contrary, Islam uplifted women at a time when they were buried alive and treated as property in pre-Islamic Arabia.
