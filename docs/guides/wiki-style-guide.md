## 🎯 **Overview**

This style guide provides comprehensive guidelines for creating, editing, and managing wiki content on IslamWiki. Following these guidelines ensures consistency, quality, and accessibility across all wiki pages.

## 📝 **Content Creation Guidelines**

### **Page Titles**

#### **Title Format**
- **Use Title Case**: Capitalize the first letter of each major word
- **Be Descriptive**: Clearly indicate the page content
- **Keep Concise**: Aim for 3-8 words maximum
- **Avoid Abbreviations**: Use full words when possible

#### **Good Title Examples**
✅ **Correct:**
- "The Golden Age of Islamic Civilization"
- "Islamic Contributions to Mathematics and Science"
- "The Art of Islamic Calligraphy and Manuscripts"
- "Prophet Muhammad's Teachings on Ethics and Morality"

❌ **Incorrect:**
- "golden age of islam" (lowercase)
- "Islamic Math" (too abbreviated)
- "Calligraphy" (too vague)
- "Prophet Muhammad's Teachings on Ethics and Morality and How They Apply to Modern Life" (too long)

#### **Title Guidelines by Content Type**
- **Articles**: Descriptive, topic-focused titles
- **Biographies**: "Name - Brief Description" format
- **Historical Events**: "Event Name (Year)" format
- **Concepts**: Clear, definition-style titles
- **How-to Guides**: "How to [Action]" format

### **Content Structure**

#### **Standard Page Layout**
```markdown
# Page Title

## Overview
Brief introduction and summary of the topic.

## Main Content Sections
### Section 1
Content for the first main section.

### Section 2
Content for the second main section.

## Key Points
- Important point 1
- Important point 2
- Important point 3

## Related Topics
Links to related pages and categories.

## References
Sources and citations for the content.
```

#### **Section Headings**
- **H1 (#)**: Page title only
- **H2 (##)**: Main sections
- **H3 (###)**: Subsections
- **H4 (####)**: Sub-subsections (use sparingly)

#### **Content Organization**
1. **Start with Overview**: Brief introduction (2-3 sentences)
2. **Logical Flow**: Organize content from general to specific
3. **Use Subsections**: Break long content into digestible chunks
4. **End with Summary**: Recap key points and next steps

### **Writing Style**

#### **Tone and Voice**
- **Professional but Accessible**: Clear, informative, engaging
- **Respectful**: Maintain Islamic values and respect
- **Objective**: Present facts without bias
- **Inclusive**: Use inclusive language and examples

#### **Language Guidelines**
- **Clear and Concise**: Avoid jargon and complex sentences
- **Active Voice**: Use active voice when possible
- **Consistent Terminology**: Use consistent terms throughout
- **Proper Grammar**: Follow standard English grammar rules

#### **Islamic Content Guidelines**
- **Respectful References**: Use proper Islamic titles and honorifics
- **Accurate Information**: Ensure historical and religious accuracy
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Respect diverse Islamic traditions
- **Source Verification**: Cite reliable Islamic sources

## 🔤 **Text Formatting**

### **Markdown Usage**

#### **Basic Formatting**
```markdown
**Bold text** for emphasis
*Italic text* for foreign words or titles
`Code` for technical terms
> Blockquote for important statements
```

#### **Lists**
```markdown
- Unordered list item
- Another item
  - Sub-item with indentation
  - Another sub-item

1. Ordered list item
2. Second item
3. Third item
```

#### **Links and References**
```markdown
[Link Text](URL)
[Internal Page](wiki-page-slug)
[External Source](https://example.com)
```

#### **Tables**
```markdown
| Column 1 | Column 2 | Column 3 |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Data 1   | Data 2   | Data 3   |
| Data 4   | Data 5   | Data 6   |
```

### **Special Formatting**

#### **Islamic Terms**
- **Arabic Terms**: Use *italics* for Arabic words
- **Transliteration**: Provide English transliteration
- **Pronunciation**: Include pronunciation guides when helpful
- **Definitions**: Define terms on first use

#### **Examples**
```markdown
*Salah* (صلاة) - Islamic prayer performed five times daily
*Zakat* (زكاة) - Obligatory charitable giving
*Hajj* (حج) - Annual pilgrimage to Mecca
```

#### **Dates and Time**
- **Islamic Calendar**: Include both Hijri and Gregorian dates
- **Format**: "15 Ramadan 1445 AH (March 25, 2024 CE)"
- **Centuries**: Use "7th century CE" or "1st century AH"

#### **Names and Titles**
- **Prophet Muhammad**: Use full title on first mention
- **Historical Figures**: Include full names and titles
- **Scholars**: Use proper titles and honorifics

## 🏷️ **Categorization and Tagging**

### **Category Guidelines**

#### **Category Selection**
- **Primary Category**: Choose the most relevant category
- **Secondary Categories**: Add 1-2 additional categories if relevant
- **Avoid Over-categorization**: Don't add categories just for the sake of it

#### **Category Types**
1. **Thematic**: Islamic History, Islamic Sciences, Islamic Art
2. **Geographic**: Middle East, South Asia, North Africa
3. **Temporal**: Early Islamic Period, Golden Age, Modern Era
4. **Subject**: Theology, Philosophy, Literature, Architecture

#### **Category Best Practices**
- **Be Specific**: Choose specific over general categories
- **Check Existing**: Use existing categories when possible
- **Create New**: Only create new categories when necessary
- **Hierarchical**: Use parent-child category relationships

### **Tagging Guidelines**

#### **Tag Selection**
- **Relevant Tags**: Use tags that accurately describe the content
- **Consistent Naming**: Use existing tag names when possible
- **Appropriate Number**: Use 3-8 tags per page
- **Descriptive**: Tags should help users find related content

#### **Tag Types**
1. **Topics**: islamic-history, mathematics, calligraphy
2. **People**: prophet-muhammad, ibn-sina, rumi
3. **Places**: mecca, baghdad, cordoba
4. **Concepts**: tawhid, sharia, sufism
5. **Time Periods**: golden-age, medieval, modern

#### **Tag Formatting**
- **Lowercase**: Use all lowercase letters
- **Hyphenated**: Separate words with hyphens
- **Descriptive**: Make tags self-explanatory
- **Consistent**: Use consistent naming conventions

## 🖼️ **Media and Images**

### **Image Guidelines**

#### **Image Selection**
- **Relevant**: Images should directly relate to the content
- **High Quality**: Use clear, high-resolution images
- **Appropriate**: Ensure images are culturally appropriate
- **Licensed**: Use properly licensed or public domain images

#### **Image Placement**
- **Contextual**: Place images near relevant text
- **Balanced**: Don't overload pages with too many images
- **Responsive**: Ensure images work on all devices
- **Accessible**: Include descriptive alt text

#### **Image Captions**
```markdown
![Alt text](image-url "Caption text")

Example:
![Islamic calligraphy example](calligraphy.jpg "Beautiful example of Thuluth calligraphy from the 15th century")
```

### **Media Types**

#### **Images**
- **Photographs**: Historical sites, artifacts, people
- **Illustrations**: Diagrams, maps, charts
- **Calligraphy**: Islamic art and writing
- **Architecture**: Mosques, palaces, buildings

#### **Videos and Audio**
- **Educational Content**: Lectures, demonstrations
- **Cultural Content**: Music, recitations
- **Documentary**: Historical footage
- **Interactive**: Virtual tours, animations

## 📖 **Citations and References**

### **Citation Guidelines**

#### **When to Cite**
- **Direct Quotes**: Always cite direct quotations
- **Facts and Figures**: Cite statistical or factual information
- **Historical Events**: Cite historical sources
- **Controversial Claims**: Provide multiple sources for disputed information

#### **Citation Format**
```markdown
## References

1. **Author Name**, *Book Title* (Publisher, Year), Page Number
2. **Author Name**, "Article Title," *Journal Name* (Year), Page Range
3. **Website Name**, "Page Title," URL (Accessed: Date)
4. **Organization Name**, *Report Title* (Year), URL
```

#### **Source Types**
1. **Primary Sources**: Original documents, artifacts, eyewitness accounts
2. **Secondary Sources**: Books, articles, documentaries
3. **Academic Sources**: Peer-reviewed journals, university publications
4. **Reliable Websites**: Established institutions, respected organizations

### **Reference Examples**

#### **Book Citation**
```markdown
1. **Jonathan Bloom** and **Sheila Blair**, *Islamic Arts* (Phaidon Press, 1997), 45-67
```

#### **Journal Article**
```markdown
2. **Ahmed Al-Rahim**, "The Golden Age of Islamic Science," *Journal of Islamic Studies* 15, no. 2 (2004): 234-256
```

#### **Website Citation**
```markdown
3. **Metropolitan Museum of Art**, "Islamic Art and Architecture," https://www.metmuseum.org (Accessed: January 20, 2025)
```
